About Mid Autumn Festival

Also known as Mooncake festival, the Mid-Autumn Festival is a cultural festive celebrated by the Chinese in China, Taiwan, Malaysia, and Singapore. It is celebrated every year on August 15 of the Lunar calendar. During the festive, members of a family will reunite together and enjoy the festive together. Usually, there would be a moon-viewing party where people would enjoy the beautiful full moon while sipping some tea and eating some moon cakes (Tan., n.d). However in Taiwan, people would usually go barbeque with their family or friends to celebrate this festive (OFTaiwan, n.d).


Mooncakes

Mooncakes have a long history, with it being used as a sacrifice to the moon gooddess as a way to "thank them", a way to communicate with the locals to rebel against the Mongols during the oppresive Yuan Dynasty. Since then, the Mooncake Festival was originated, it was consumed and shared among families and friends as a way to celebrate the festival. (Jiang. F, 2022).


Moon Sightseeing

Since the Mooncake Festival falls on the 15th of the 8th month of the lunar calender, which is also a full moon day. This full moon is also known as the "Harvest Moon". It is the quickest full moon to rise above the horizon, enabling many to be able to sightsee the moon as early as sunset. (Jiang. F, 2022).
